    Freedom Index Formula

    Based on Freedom House methodology. Surveillance and censorship apply negative multiplicative pressure; regulation and transparency provide partial mitigation.

    Freedom = 100
    - (surveillance × 0.45)
    - (censorship × 0.40)
    + (regulation × 0.12)
    + (transparency × 0.08)

    Time decay: high surveillance creates institutional lock-in
    → additional -0.8 per year if surveillance > 60
    Basis: Freedom House Political Rights + Civil Liberties scoring rubric · V-Dem liberal component index
    Economic Stability Formula

    Automation creates a productivity gain offset by inequality and unemployment. Welfare and regulation dampen negative effects with realistic lag.

    Economy = 50
    + (automation × 0.20) [productivity gain]
    - (automation × 0.15) [displacement cost]
    + (welfare × 0.25) [safety net buffer]
    - (gini_penalty) [inequality drag]

    gini_penalty = automation > 60 ? (automation-60) × 0.4 : 0

    Short term (t=0): automation boost dominates
    Long term (t>5): displacement and inequality accumulate
    Basis: World Bank labor market analysis · Acemoglu & Restrepo (2019) "Automation and New Tasks" · OECD "Future of Work"
    Public Trust Formula
    Trust = 60
    - (surveillance × 0.30) [chilling effect]
    - (censorship × 0.25) [perceived manipulation]
    + (welfare × 0.20) [social contract]
    + (transparency × 0.15) [accountability]
    - (automation_shock) [economic anxiety]

    automation_shock = max(0, (automation - 50) × 0.15)

    Scenario modifier: authoritarian context
    → surveillance has 1.4× impact on trust erosion
    Basis: Edelman Trust Barometer 2024 · Pew Research "AI and Human Enhancement" 2023 · Levi & Stoker (2000) trust theory
    Limitations & Caveats
    This is a policy modeling tool, not a predictive simulation. Formulas encode empirical tendencies from research literature, not deterministic laws. Real outcomes depend on implementation quality, historical path dependencies, and contingent events not captured here.
    The SIR model for misinformation is adapted from epidemiological models. It captures spread dynamics but does not model individual psychology, algorithmic recommendation effects, or cross-platform dynamics at full fidelity.
    All preset regime values are approximations calibrated to published index scores. They represent governance patterns, not identical reproductions of real measurements which change year to year.
